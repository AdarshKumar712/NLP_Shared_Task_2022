International Batuta Awards Ceremony to be Held in Cuba in 2020

Published: 2020.01.08 - 09:31:03  /  web@renciclopedia.icrt.cu  /  Redacción Digital

The president of the Latin Music Institute (ILM), Daniel Martin, recently announced that he plans to hold an edition of the Batuta Awards this year in Havana, Cuba, in November.

The young cultural promoter works with the conviction that music heals and unites people, and therefore takes every duty as an opportunity to build bridges.

The most recent responsibility granted was to organize the Batuta Awards, homologous to the Grammy but granted in recognition of world personalities of classical and concert music.

"Classical music, until now, in many cases, has been a field of battle of egos, but in times when humanity is perhaps a little more spiritual, more interconnected, the ideal moment has come to be able to place classical musicians in one place," he said in an exclusive interview with Prensa Latina.

In the first edition of the Batuta Awards, icons such as Spanish Plácido Domingo, British Michael Nyman, Cuban Leo Brouwer, and Edesio Alejandro, Mexican Enrique Bátiz and Carlos Esteva, Swedish Daniel Lozakovich (a young man considered to be a musical prodigy), among others, were awarded.

This event, which unites them all, will be attended by great personalities, said Martín, who in 2019 received the Benito Juárez Order in Mexico for their contributions to Latin American culture, as well as an Honoris Causa Ph. D. from two center for higher studies, the International Seagull Award and an Honorary Mention from the University of Puebla.

In this capital, the ceremony would take place after the 27th Havana International Ballet Festival and prior to the 42nd International Festival of New Latin American Cinema, so as not to coincide with other great events.

Martin intends to bring together artists from North America, Europe, Latin America, and the Caribbean, among others, and from the list of honorees, he has brought forward Cuban maestros Guido López Gavilán, Enrique Pérez Mesa, Zenaida Romeu, Ulises Aquino, and Frank Fernández.

"The awards gala would include performances and we would, of course, invite the great maestros to be part of the shows," he said.

In his opinion, the ideal place to present the Batuta Awards is the García Lorca hall of the Alicia Alonso Gran Teatro de La Habana and Martín thanked the Ministry of Culture and the Cuban Music Institute for their support.

According to Martín, his management of the Batuta Awards will try to do justice in life to many great maestros, and for this reason, the award could have several itinerant editions in the same year, at least in its first five years.

Afterward, it could be held annually or, perhaps, one in Asia, one in Europe and one in America, in respect for such wealth, he said.

This year, Martín will support the production of a documentary to celebrate the centennial of the ILM in 2021, under the direction of Cuban musician Edesio Alejandro, who is the ILM's cultural ambassador and an active member of the Hollywood Film Academy.

In addition, he will produce a song performed by several Cuban musicians and Mexican singer Lila Down, a multi-award winning folklorist who recently became the first artist from her country to be named cultural ambassador of the ILM.

Currently, the ILM is growing inward, I love this idea, we are trying not only to be in the big cities but to reach municipalities, and perhaps more intricate regions, we are doing studies of folklore.

"I think we should look inward, toward our ancestors, and be very proud of that," Martín concluded.
