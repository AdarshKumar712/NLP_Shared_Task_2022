    Why Apple may have to ‘thank’ Donald Trump for its first store in India

    Why Apple may have to ‘thank’ Donald Trump for its first store in India
    The 1st Apple store in India is set to open in 2021. Apple CEO Tim Cook said that the company got a special approval as the govt of India wanted Apple to work with a local partner. The approval came after lobbying efforts by the Trump administration. “We wouldn’t be a very good partner in retail. We like to do things our way,” Cook said.
    TIMESOFINDIA.COM | Updated: Mar 3, 2020, 10:15AM IST
    For the longest time, Apple fans — and industry watchers — have been keenly waiting for the company to open its first retail store in India. Last week, Apple CEO Tim Cook officially confirmed that his company is on track to open its first store in India sometime in 2021.In an interview, Cook said that the company got a special approval as the government of India wanted Apple to work with a local partner. “We wouldn’t be a very good partner in retail. We like to do things our way,” Cook said.


    Now it seems like Apple will not have to find a partner and it’s all thanks to the role US president Donald Trump played in it. Cook in an interview with Fox News said that the Trump administration did lobby for Apple to get the ball rolling. “The administration worked on this with the Indian government and that change has been made,” Cook said in the interview.


    Elaborating further on its India retail plans, Cook said that in 2020 Apple will get its online store ready. The first Apple store, however, will be up and running sometime in 2021. No further details about location or the exact timelines have been revealed but it’s clear that Trump did play a pivotal role in it.


    Speaking of Trump, the US president has had an interesting — to say the least — equation with Apple and its CEO. In the past, Trump has praised and criticised Apple in equal measure. In an FBI investigation of a shooting incident, Trump said that Apple should fully cooperate with the authorities, something which the company said it was doing. Trump has also said that he has had at times worked on tariffs to ‘help’ Apple in its fight against Samsung. And of course, there was the infamous incident where he referred to Tim Cook as Tim ‘Apple’.
