MiradasDoc to spot budding directors 

Posted: 11:49, February 11, 2020 by TenerifeNews

The international film festival MiradasDoc is currently taking place in Guia de Isora and could well lead to a new career for budding film-makers.

Judges of international acclaim will be looking at a series of documentaries and reality cinema on a social theme and awarding prizes for the best. For many previous winners, it has meant a stepping stone into the huge world of film-making.
The festival opened on February 1st and continues until the 8th.
“We are interested in documentaries capable of offering a look towards the present from new creative languages,” explains Alejandro Krawietz, director of MiradasDoc with David Baute.
In the 55 films of this year’s official section, topics such as wild Europe, gender issues, feminism, Africa, childhood and culture appear. In all of them, without exception, what stands out is the unusual look that the authors offer about realities throughout the world.
Alejandro Krawietz points out that this abundance of themes depends every year on the eyes of the filmmakers, on what they were able to see. “But they end up composing a viewpoint towards deep actuality, towards the great transversal themes.”
Films have been submitted from as far afield as Serbia, Italy, Switzerland, Germany, Portugal, Argentina and of course Spain and the Canaries.
The competitions are for best documentary feature film, best short film, best first and best national opera. The documentaries will be exhibited in the Auditorium of the municipality of southern Tenerife until February 8th.
