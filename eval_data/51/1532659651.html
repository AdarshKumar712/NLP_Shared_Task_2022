Girls basketball: PVHS season ends after one round at state

HIGHLANDS RANCH -- Prairie View's stay in the state 5A girls basketball tournament lasted one round.

KATHY SCHNEIDER
Prairie View’s Kaylee Deshazer (24) blocks a shot from Mountain Vista’s Berit Brookfield during the girls class 5A Alice Barron Region first-round playoff game Feb. 25 at Mountain Vista High School. The ThunderHawks lost 80-45.
Buy this photo

Even so, first-year coach Sam Mackall was pleased the T-Hawks advanced to the postseason.

"We had a new team this year. A lot of the girls didn't play varsity last year," Mackall said. "We put it together. We put some things in place. We had some ups and downs. But I'm proud of what the young ladies brought and what the young ladies accomplished. They had to learn me as I learn them. Sometimes, you don't have enough time to make it happen.

"We made a little run at the end," Mackall continued. PVHS beat Adams City and Westminster high schools in successive games to close out the regular season The wins snapped a four-game losing streak at the time. "We wish a few games would have went differently. We wish we had a higher seed. We would have met them in the first, second or third round. I'm so proud of what my young ladies did."

As for the game itself, Mountain Vista High School scored 43 of the first half's last 57 points and went on to an 80-45 win on the Golden Eagles' court Feb. 25.

"It was one of those nights where things just don't go your way, except for the first five to seven minutes," said PVHS' first-year coach, Sam Mackall. "They had great defense, and we'd known that coming into the game. We played with them on their game, and that was the biggest difference. They are, rightfully so, going on to the next round. They deserved it."

MVHS faces Broomfield in the second round Friday, Feb. 28. PVHS finished the season with a record of 10-14 and its sixth straight state playoff berth.

The teams traded the first 18 points of the game. The Golden Eagles scored the next 13 in a row to pull away. By the end of the half, the margin was 29 points. 

Mackenzie Gamble led the victors with 22 points, including four, 3-pointers. Kariss Cash added 15. Shantell Hafner tossed in 14. PVHS' top scorer was Micah Jewett, She finished with 17 points, including five from long range. Lilianna Ortega added 11.

PVHS loses its starting five to graduation.

"We will make sure we get the team ready to. We just need to work on spring, summer and fall," Mackall said. "My girls didn't give up all the way to the end. My team fought all the way to the end."
