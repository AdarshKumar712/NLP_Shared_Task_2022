Paris Hilton’s New Documentary Reveals A ‘Raw’ Side Of Her After Years Of ‘Playing A Character’

by Stephanie Kaloi
Advertisement

Depending on how old you are and when you came of age, the name Paris Hilton might mean a few different things to you.

Advertisement

Hotel megabrand heiress, reality TV star, singer — she’s kind of done it all. Now Paris is the focus of a brand new YouTube documentary, This Is Paris.

As you might suspect, the documentary focuses on Paris herself as she grapples with establishing a new identity and brand for herself while accepting how many in the public see her. Paris is most notoriously known as one of the stars of The Simple Life, a reality show that first aired in 2003. Costarring Nicole Richie, the show followed the two children of celebrities as they navigated rural life.

Advertisement

On her role in the show, Paris explained to People, “I knew what I was doing but the whole world didn’t, so it was kind of frustrating to me to be perceived in that way because it’s not who I am at all. But it was such an amazing and entertaining show that I would just think of it as entertainment.”

A post shared by Hilton Fans💕💕 (@teamhilton) on Jan 21, 2020 at 7:08am PST

Paris went on to explain that a lot of “mean” jokes were made at her expense as a result of her time on the show, which she still grapples with. “Obviously, I’ve grown as a person. I’m an adult now, but just to be judged and have so many misconceptions based on a character that I had invented myself was frustrating.”

View this post on Instagram

A post shared by TN8 (@comunidatn8) on Jan 20, 2020 at 12:07pm PST

However, Paris also acknowledges that she reaped a lot of benefits from her time on the show. “At the same time, I built a huge brand off doing it. So, I don’t really regret it and I’m happy that people are going to finally see this film.”

View this post on Instagram

A post shared by Hilton Fans💕💕 (@teamhilton) on Jan 20, 2020 at 1:34pm PST

Paris discussed the show in detail, explaining that at the time, it was mostly just for fun. “I was having fun with it. I think, you know, I was in on the joke. People thought that’s who I really was, so sometimes it is annoying that people assume that I am kind of that, you know, blonde airhead that I was playing on the show.”

A post shared by Diane Buzzetta (@dianebuzzetta) on Jan 20, 2020 at 3:34pm PST

She added, “But I love, also, proving people wrong and any time they meet me in real life they’re always very surprised. So, it makes me happy to know that I created something that basically started an entire new generation of people.”

A post shared by Jason Bratcher (@bratcherjason) on Jan 20, 2020 at 6:16pm PST

Paris even says that filming the documentary was incredibly helpful for her. “During this film, I have discovered so much about myself that I did not even know. It was almost like a therapeutic experience. My old persona, that was who I was before when I started my brand. But now, I want to show the true boss babe that I am.”

A post shared by Aquarius Mood (@aquarius.mood) on Jan 20, 2020 at 7:53pm PST

Paris is definitely raising interesting issues about personal branding and living your life in a public way. Paris could be considered one of the original “reality” influencers who now fill the social media feeds of just about everyone.

Aaron Saidman, the executive producer of the documentary, initially had his doubts. “The early persona of Paris Hilton that we all grew up with was sort of the original impetus for why we wanted to make this documentary, because I thought I knew Paris Hilton as well just like much of the public.”

A post shared by Cooper Irons (@itscooperyall) on Jan 21, 2020 at 6:07am PST

But those thoughts were quickly turned around after he spent time with Paris. “I came to discover that I was wrong and that wasn’t really the image of her. I was reading old press articles about Paris and one of the interesting insights in all the press and all of the conversations that we’ve been having about the Hilton family for the last 20-some years is that we’ve been having this conversation with each other.”

Aaron added, “We haven’t really heard the sort of authentic inner truth from Paris herself. And if we did, it was that persona that you were referring to. So, that was why we wanted to hear what she was really like and what she was really going through.”

Paris decided to dive into the project with her whole self, leaving nothing off-limits. “When I first got approached about doing this documentary by Aaron, I was very reluctant to do it. And I didn’t really want to take the meeting, because I wasn’t ready to really show myself. So it was something very hard, because in this film I really show who I truly am.”

A post shared by parisinchanel (@parisinchanel) on Jan 20, 2020 at 4:35pm PST

She added, “I’ve been playing this character for so long. And now, even during this film, I really discovered a lot about myself and who I really am, and I wanted the world to see that there are a lot of misconceptions. And I’ve been judged based on a character that I created in the beginning of my career. And now I feel like it’s finally time that people see who the real Paris is.”

View this post on Instagram

A post shared by CA International Trade Center (@catradecenter) on Jan 20, 2020 at 4:44pm PST

This Is Paris will premiere on YouTube in May 2020. If there’s one thing we all kind of collectively adore, it’s a good comeback story. Time will tell if this attempt at taking control of the narrative surrounding her will help Paris, or if it will simply add to the collection of experiences that make her an enigma to most of us.
