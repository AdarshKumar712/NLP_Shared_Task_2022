Clayton Anderson to livestream concert to raise money for local bartenders, restaurant workers

Clayton Anderson to livestream concert to raise money for local bartenders, restaurant workers
Musician and Bedford, Indiana native, Clayton Anderson, will perform at the Pavilion at Pan Am on Thursday, April 2 to raise money for local restaurant and bar workers. (Instagram/Clayton Anderson)
Author: WTHR.com staff
Published: 7:28 AM EDT April 1, 2020
Updated: 3:11 AM EDT April 2, 2020

INDIANAPOLIS (WTHR) — Hoosier and musician Clayton Anderson will livestream a performance on Thursday to raise money for B.A.R.S. fund (Bartender and Restaurant Support) in Indianapolis.

Anderson will take the stage at the Pavilion at Pan Am Plaza on Thursday, April 2 at 6 p.m.

The concert, which is part of the Bud Light Dive Bar Tour, will be streamed to Facebook and Instagram.

View this post on Instagram

This Thursday at 6p, thanks to #BudLight we’re performing a full band set at The Pavilion Pan Am to raise money for service industry workers affected by COVID-19 in Indy. Link below to donate. Link: fundly.com/indybars

A post shared by Clayton Anderson (@claytonanderson) on Mar 31, 2020 at 4:09pm PDT


As part of the B.A.R.S. fund, local restaurant workers affected by COVID-19 can apply for up to $250 in support.

The fundraiser currently has a goal of $10,000.
