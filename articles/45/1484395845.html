First babies at St. Luke’s, Charlton; last baby at Tobey maternity unit

 

Wednesday

Jan 1, 2020 at 4:38 PM Jan 2, 2020 at 9:40 AM


NEW BEDFORD - The first baby of the New Year born at St. Luke’s Hospital in New Bedford arrived at 2:59 a.m. on Wednesday, Jan. 1, 2020. Samantha Williams of New Bedford, welcomed baby girl, Amelia Roberts, weighing 6 pounds, 11 ounces, and measuring 21 inches long. Amelia is Williams’ third child.

Cassie and Kyle Lapati of Smithfield, R.I., gave birth to a daughter, Paige Lapati, at 8:14 a.m., Jan. 1, 2020, at Charlton Memorial Hospital in Fall River. Paige is born weighing 6 pounds, 11 ounces, and measuring 19 inches long. Paige is the couple’s first child.

Both families received a $100 gift card, a plush toy and baby blanket donated by the Auxiliaries of St. Luke’s Hospital and Charlton Memorial Hospital, according to a press release. The families also received a gift basket donated by the Southcoast YMCA that includes a 1-year family membership, a baby swim class, a onesie, diapers, baby blanket, T-shirts for the parents, and a Y backpack.

There was no New Year’s baby born at Tobey Hospital’s maternity unit. Southcoast Health closed Tobey maternity on Dec. 31 over opposition from nurses, moms who had previously delivered at Tobey, and legislators, who argued that the Tobey unit provided excellent care and was needed locally.

Officials with Southcoast Health, which includes St. Luke’s and Charlton, have said that Wareham-area mothers would be able to deliver at St. Luke’s locally.
