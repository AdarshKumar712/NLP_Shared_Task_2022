                        News: 3 hours agoParker (shoulder/hamstring) was listed as limited on Wednesday's practice estimation, Daniel Oyefusi of the Miami Herald reports.

                        Spin: The Dolphins held a walk-through Wednesday, so what Parker is able to do during Thursday's session should provide a better idea of whether he has a shot to return to action Sunday against the Falcons. That the wideout was listed as limited Wednesday is encouraging, given that Parker didn't practice at all leading up to Miami's Week 6 loss to the Jaguars.

                        ESPN Fantasy Projection: Following a breakout 2019 campaign in which he posted a 72-1,202-9 receiving line en route to a WR11 fantasy campaign, Parker crashed back to earth in 2020. He missed two games, was held to a 63-793-4 line and finished 40th in fantasy points. He has now missed at least one game (13 total) and finished 40th or worse in fantasy points in five of his six seasons. Parker will have more competition for targets this season after Miami signed Will Fuller V and drafted Jaylen Waddle, and the team's QB situation is a question mark with unproven Tua Tagovailoa under center. The 28-year-old Parker will still play a sizable role, but there's enough risk here that he's best-valued as a flex option.
