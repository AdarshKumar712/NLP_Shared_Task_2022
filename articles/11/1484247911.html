RI couple welcomes Fall River's first new baby of the year

 
By Amanda Burke

Wednesday

Jan 1, 2020 at 6:31 PM Jan 1, 2020 at 7:16 PM


FALL RIVER — As the clock struck midnight and a new decade began, Cassie and Kyle Lapati were in their car, driving from their home in Smithfield, Rhode Island, to Charlton Memorial Hospital for the birth of their daughter.

“We didn’t get to see the ball drop. But it’s a good way to start the year,” Cassie said as she cradled her newborn, Paige, in the maternity wing of Charlton Memorial Hospital.

At 6 lbs, 11 ounces and 19 inches long, Paige came into the world at 8:14 a.m. on New Year's Day, making her the first baby born at the Fall River hospital this year, and this decade.

Cassie’s due date was Jan. 2, she said. But at a doctor’s appointment on Tuesday, she was told her first child may make an entrance as early as that very evening. So the couple stayed close to home.

“My doctor said, 'You’re probably going to go tonight or tomorrow,' so we just kind of hung at the house with the dog. I was literally playing fetch with the dog while having contractions,” she said.

Paige’s arrival on New Year's Day may not have come as a surprise. But one big unknown remained until the moment their child was born — whether they were having a boy or girl. The couple, who have been together for 15 years, came to the hospital prepared with a list of possible baby names.

“We finally just decided on Paige five minutes ago,” Cassie said at about 3 p.m. “We didn’t know what we were having. It was a surprise. And we were in agreement that we wouldn’t pick it until we met them, and figure it out from there.”

Cassie and Kyle met when they were students at Ponaganset High School in Rhode Island. They married seven years ago and moved into a lakeside house in Smithfield, said Cassie.

“We had our careers, and got our home, and finally we got to the point where we said it’s time for the next step,” said Cassie, who works as an education sales representative.

Kyle marveled at how alert his newborn was just a few hours after birth, and Cassie pointed out the resemblance her daughter bears to her husband.

“It’s so funny, in the ultrasounds we thought she looked like me, but when she came out she looked like you,” she told Kyle.

Paige’s eyes grew heavy, fluttering open shut, and her was head wrapped in a bow like a present.

Asked what she’s looking forward to the most during the decade to come, Cassie said, “everything, all the experiences of having a kid.”

“Our lives are completely different and changed,” she said.

"It's a new normal. Ready or not, here she is. It's going to be a busy 2020." Kyle said with a smile.
