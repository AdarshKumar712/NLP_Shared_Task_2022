Three Kings Celebration to benefit families who left Puerto Rico after Hurricane Maria

Dave Berman, Florida Today Published 10:56 a.m. ET Jan. 1, 2020
CLOSE
CONNECTTWEETLINKEDINEMAILMORE
Buy Photo

United Third Bridge President Samuel Lopez says about 285 families relocated from Puerto Rico to Brevard County following Hurricane Maria. (Photo: J.D. GALLOP/FLORIDA TODAY FILE PHOTO)

A Three Kings Celebration this weekend will focus on helping Latin families who settled in Brevard County following devastation on the island caused by Hurricane Maria in September 2017.

The United Third Bridge Puerto Rico Relief Fund's third-annual Three Kings Celebration and children's party will be held from noon to 7 p.m. Sunday at the VFW/Knights of Columbus building at 2150 Dairy Road in Melbourne.

United Third Bridge President Samuel Lopez said about 285 families relocated from Puerto Rico to Brevard County following Hurricane Maria.

Sunday's event is targeted to help children in those families with clothing and gifts, and is part of a larger effort by United Third Bridge, a Melbourne-based civil rights, educational and cultural nonprofit organization.

A number of local organizations and businesses are sponsoring Sunday's event, including Brevard Adult Education, Eastern Florida State College and Seafarer Exploration.

Buy Photo

These were among the attendees in last year's Three Kings Celebration organized by the United Third Bridge Puerto Rico Relief Fund. (Photo: TIM SHORTT/FLORIDA TODAY FILE PHOTO)

The event will feature food, beverages, games, music by DJ Louis and the singing of Christmas carols.

Lopez said the event is open to all local families, not just those who have relocated from Puerto Rico.

The United Third Bridge Puerto Rico Relief Fund is focusing on helping families that relocated from Puerto Rico secure permanent housing and helping their children adjust to the local school system.

For details on how people can help the Puerto Rico Relief Fund, contact the organization's volunteer director, Margarita Chavez, at 321-863-5165.

    Three Kings Day — which also is known in Christianity as Epiphany — marks the culmination of the Christmas season. The actual day is Monday, Jan. 6.

    Three Kings Day — a major holiday in Puerto Rico — spotlights the "Three Kings of the Epiphany," the men who followed the star to Bethlehem, to offer the baby Jesus presents of gold, frankincense and myrrh 12 days after he was born. In Spanish cultures, Los Reyes Magos come to people's homes on the night of Jan. 5 (the eve of Three Kings Day) to bring them presents.
