Springfield Police Department modifies how it will interact with public

Wes Johnson, Springfield News-Leader Published 7:15 p.m. CT March 23, 2020
CLOSE
CONNECTTWEETLINKEDINCOMMENTEMAILMORE
Buy Photo

File art (Photo: News-Leader)

All of the News-Leader's coverage of coronavirus is being provided for free to our readers. Please consider supporting local journalism by subscribing atNews-Leader.com/subscribe.

The Springfield Police Department announced some changes in the way it will deal with calls for help and contact with the public because of the coronavirus outbreak.

In a news release, the department says officers remain on patrol and will continue to respond to calls for service.

However, in compliance with personal distance guidelines, people who are ablewill be asked to meet officers outside of their residence or business and maintain a minimum distance of six feet from the officer during the interaction.

If you call police, dispatchers might ask additional questions to determine the appropriate level of police response.

Each incident will be evaluated by the telecommunicators, police supervisors, and responding officers to determine the type of police response you may receive. You may be directed to call information into the police department, an officer may contact you by telephone or you may be instructed to complete an online report.

These efforts are designed to limit contact as much as possible while still providing police service and maintaining a healthy community and department whilefollowing updated health guidelines.

Questions about the changes can be directed to SPD's non-emergency phone line, 417-864-1810.
