    Tottenham are being used by Barcelona and Philippe Coutinho

    by Rucker Haringey 2 days ago Follow @RuckerHaringey
    • Tweet
    • Share
    • x
    • Pin
    • Comment

    Philippe Coutinho would be an ideal replacement for Christian Eriksen at Tottenham, but there’s no way Spurs will pay Barcelona’s asking price. 

    In a perfect world, Tottenham would be able to replace Christian Eriksen with an established star this January. Philippe Coutinho absolutely would fit the bill. Unfortunately for Spurs, Spanish reports linking the Brazilian live wire with a sensational move to North London are purely a ploy by Barcelona officials to drive his transfer value through the roof.

    Eldesmarque regards Barcelona selling Coutinho for a small fortune as the key to the La Liga giants’ hopes of bringing Neymar back to Spain. To that end, Barcelona are asking a number of Premier League clubs to pay upwards of £100 million to secure his services next summer.

    Chelsea, Manchester United and Tottenham are the three English clubs mentioned by name in the Spanish publication. To put it bluntly, one of these clubs is not like the other when it comes to transfer dealings. Spurs have never spent anything close to £100 million on a single player. The chances of them doing so while paying for a state-of-the-art new stadium are almost non-existent.

    It’s obvious that Barcelona officials are desperate to keep Coutinho’s transfer stock at its zenith. It’s hard to call his time in Spain anything other than a flop. He failed to show the sparkling attacking characteristics that made him a star at Liverpool during his brief time on the pitch with Lionel Messi and his star-studded team.

    Fortunately for Barcelona, his time at Bayern Munich does seem to be going better than his cameos in Spain. He hasn’t fully regained the stardom he enjoyed at Anfield, but he’s been an important cog for the German giants. They might like to retain him for tactical reasons, but it’s highly unlikely Bayern will stump the fee required to acquire him on a permanent basis.

    Next: Tottenham may let Vertonghen leave for free

    As such, it’s imperative for Barcelona officials to keep big Premier League clubs interested in Coutinho’s services. Tottenham are never going to win the race for the talented No. 10, but their “inclusion” in the bidding will help push clubs like Chelsea or Manchester United to bid against themselves. That’s the game Barcelona are playing here. Spurs will not be a real factor for Coutinho this summer.
