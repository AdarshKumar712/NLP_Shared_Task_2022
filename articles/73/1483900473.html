YOU'RE KIDDING

Furious mum bursts into tears after husband tells three-year-old daughter he loves her ‘more than anything’

  • Lydia Hawken
  • 1 Jan 2020, 11:05
  • Updated: 1 Jan 2020, 11:06

WHETHER we admit to it or not, we ALL know what it's like to be jealous of that hilarious new female friend your partner won't stop banging on about.

But while it's pretty natural to feel possessive of your partner around other women, one mum has sparked fierce debate online after she admitted to being jealous of her three-year-old daughter - and it's certainly a first.

Sharing his side of the story on Reddit's "Am I the A*****" forum, the husband explained how his daughter worries he'll "stop loving her" when she does something naughty.

He wrote: "Today she bumped a glass off the table and started crying. She asked if I still love her and I said “I love you more than anything” and gave her a kiss."

Although we're sure this scene would melt most mothers' hearts, this man's wife had a different reaction entirely.

He continued: "My wife was watching and stormed off. I made sure my daughter was fine and cleaned up the glass and went to go see what was going on."

When he went to comfort his wife, the man found that she was "crying and [called him] an a****** who doesn't love her."

He added: "I guess I have 2 irrational 3 years olds now. I said of course I love her and asked why she was wigging out."

After delving into it further, the mum confessed that it was the phrase "more than anything" is what bothered her most.

"She said that because I said the above phrase to my daughter, it meant she didn’t mean as much to me," he added.

She said that because I said the above phrase to my daughter, it meant she didn’t mean as much to me. Is this really something I’m not supposed to say?

Unsure how to comfort his wife, the man concluded: "Is this really something I’m not supposed to say? My mother said it to me, and my dad didn’t freak out."

Unsurprisingly, the dilemma sparked fierce debate in the comments - and many argued that the mum was showing signs of postnatal depression.

One replied: "Dude... [you're] not the a******. Your wife needs therapy STAT! That’s not a normal response."

Commenting on the little girl's fear of her father not loving her, another added: "Children are super perceptive - your daughter could be unconsciously imitating that anxious behaviour from her mother if breakdowns like this are common. Please consider therapy for them both."

Meanwhile, a third weighed in with: "I’m not saying I disagree because therapy is great, but also keep in mind how tired and emotionally drained a lot of parents are.

"It’s pretty common for people who are tired to overreact emotionally to minor things that usually wouldn’t bother them."
