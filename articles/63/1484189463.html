NH man submitted nearly $111K in false Medicaid claims

 

Tuesday

Dec 31, 2019 at 9:03 AM


CONCORD, N.H. (AP) ï¿½ The owner of a company that provides in-home care to Medicaid participants in New Hampshire was sentenced to at least 1 1/2 years in prison for submitting nearly $111,000 in false claims for the program funds.

Richard Gaudette, 63, of Danville, was sentenced Monday in Merrimack County Superior Court. Enforcement of the sentence was stayed pending his appeal. He was convicted in October.

Gaudette was the owner of the Happy at Home Care and Assistance company. Prosecutors said over a four-year period, Gaudette claimed and received reimbursement for in-home, personal care services that were never actually provided, which included periods when Happy at Home clients were not at home, but instead were in hospitals, in nursing homes, or deceased.

The attorney general office's Medicaid Fraud Control Unit investigated and prosecuted the case following a referral from the Program Integrity Unit of the New Hampshire Department of Health and Human Services.
