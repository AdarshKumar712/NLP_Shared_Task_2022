    New minister joins spiritual center

    The Rev. Lin Nowicki is the new senior minister at United Life Center for Spiritual Living, 3332 N Meridian.

    Nowicki accepted the center's letter of call and began serving in her new post on March 1.

    The center, a member of Golden, Colorado-based Centers for Spiritual Living, conducted a yearlong search before Nowicki, of Sante Fe, New Mexico, offered her acceptance of the senior minister's post. The center held a reception after her inaugural address to the community. She was a member of the Santa Fe Center for Spiritual Living, is licensed as a religious science practitioner she has been teaching certificated Science of Mind classes since the mid-1990’s. As her center's delegate to the Santa Fe Interfaith Leadership Alliance, she particularly appreciated participating in their monthly interfaith study group.

    Nowicki is also an artist and writer. Her paintings hang in homes and offices from Washington, D.C., to Norway and Spain, and her articles and short stories have been published in periodicals as diverse as The Christian Science Monitor, St. Anthony Messenger, Mother Earth News and Science of Mind Magazine. She is mother of two adult children.

    Sunday morning meditation is 10:30 a.m. at the center, followed by Sunday Celebration Service at 11 a.m.

    Methodists shorten conference

    Instead of a three-day gathering May 25-27, The Oklahoma United Methodist Conference's Annual Conference will be shortened to a one-day gathering May 27 and a two-day gathering Nov. 20-21.

    Conference leaders said memorial, retirement and commissioning services will take place in May at St. Luke’s United Methodist Church in Oklahoma City, and an ordination service will take place in November at United Methodist Church of the Servant in Oklahoma City. If health concerns prevent a meeting May 27, the one-day gathering will be rescheduled for June 20 at Church of the Servant. Business sessions for clergy and laity will be scheduled during both events.

    The tiered schedule includes a two-week final notification regarding whether an annual conference date will be rescheduled. The two-part meeting is intended to give conference leadership flexibility so they can respond to public health concerns in a timely manner.

    "Decisions made during Annual Conference impact every church and ministry in the state, as well as the health and welfare of our clergy," the Rev. Derrek Belase, the conference's director of connectional ministry, said in a news release. "We ask for all our ministry leaders to offer one another grace as we work to move forward together during this difficult time."

    Archdiocese creates online events list

    (Story continued below...)

    The Archdiocese of Oklahoma City has compiled a list of daily and Sunday online Masses for churches in the archdiocese.

    Most of the services are broadcast on Facebook Live and archived on the parish YouTube channel.

    The Mass times and prayers included in the list are temporary and are being offered during the suspension of in-person Masses at all parishes in the archdiocese, in the wake of the coronavirus outbreak.

    The list may be found on a special webpage, archokc.org/health, which was created to post all information related to COVID-19.
