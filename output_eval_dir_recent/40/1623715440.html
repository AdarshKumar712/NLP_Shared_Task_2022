    Liverpool take a knee to protest George Floyd's death

    Premier League Using #BlackLivesMatter

      • MARCA
      • Compartir en Facebook
      • Compartir en Twitter
      • Enviar por email
    • 01/06/2020 19:59 CEST
    En Español

    Sancho's message after scoring: Justice for George Floyd

    Liverpool's squad have posed for a photo in support of George Floyd, who was killed by police in Minneapolis.

    The current Premier League leaders and Champions League holders gathered around the centre circle at Anfield, taking a knee to show their support.

    Unity is strength #BlackLivesMatterpic.twitter.com/pLhzelu7n5

    ? Adrián San Miguel (@AdriSanMiguel) June 1, 2020

    Unity is strength #BlackLivesMatterpic.twitter.com/wWCi2iaBBt

    ? Virgil van Dijk (@VirgilvDijk) June 1, 2020

    The gesture was popularised in 2016 by former NFL star Colin Kaepernick, who knelt during the USA's national anthem.

    A number of Liverpool players shared the image along with the hashtag #BlackLivesMatter.
