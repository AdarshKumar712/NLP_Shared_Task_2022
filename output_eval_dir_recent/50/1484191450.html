SOCSD gears up for kindergarten tours, registration

  • By CHARLIE BENTON Starkville Daily News
  • Dec 30, 2019
  • Dec 30, 2019 Updated 5 hrs ago

  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Facebook

Families will soon be able to register their students for kindergarten at Sudduth Elementary School and West Elementary School.

Registration will begin early in the new year, starting on March 4. Registration can be completed online. Small group tours will begin Jan. 22, and will also be open to parents and future kindergarteners to see the school and watch teachers in the classroom. Tours will be available until the first week in March.

“We’ll have small group tours for groups of people to come and see the school and see some classrooms and ask questions before kindergarten registration,” said Sudduth Principal Morgan Abraham.

A link for registration will go live on the district’s website once registration opens, registration can also be completed at the schools during the same period. A final push for registration will take place July 20-24 prior to the school year starting in August.

Families are required to provide their child’s immunization records, birth certificate and two proofs of residency for registration.

“Once they complete the online registration form for kindergarten, they can take their birth certificate, two proofs of residency and immunization form to Sudduth or to West any day of the week during normal office hours,” said SOCSD Public Information Officer Nicole Thomas.

A kindergarten open house will also take place on March 3, offering parents another chance to visit Sudduth prior to the start of kindergarten. While those who come to the open house will not get to watch teachers in action like they would on the tours, they will be able to meet all 18 kindergarten teachers.

Abraham discussed some of the opportunities kindergarteners have at Sudduth, including classes like music, library, physical education, makerspace and art. Makerspace activities can also take place in the classroom.

“Kindergarten is very fast paced,” Abraham said. “It starts with letters and sounds, and we try to have kids reading sentences and comprehending questions when asked by the time they head to first grade. We focus on the whole child, because some students have come to kindergarten and have been home with a family member or been to a daycare for some amount of time or to a preschool, but kindergarten is a different type of setting for them.”
