Letter: Nursery at prison helps inmates’ rehabilitation

 

Wednesday

Jan 1, 2020 at 4:11 AM


I was touched by the Sunday Dispatch article “It takes a village” about a new nursery building at the Ohio Reformatory for Women for incarcerated new mothers who are learning the skills they need to become better parents. The "Hope House," as it's called, is fully equipped with kitchen and laundry services. It even has an on-site medical office for visits with the pediatrician.


As a medical student, I found this to be incredibly important for the health of the mother and the development of the child.


The Hope House is more of a rehabilitation center than a prison and it's producing great results. So, why isn't every prison in Ohio more like it? Ohio is in desperate need of criminal justice reform, considering our track record on incarceration. In 2011, Ohio was the first state to sell a prison to a private company, according to the ACLU of Ohio. The prison population grew rapidly, causing a massive overcrowding issue.


Today, Ohio incarcerates almost five times as many people as the entire United Kingdom and a disproportionate amount of them are black, according to the Prison Policy Initiative. In 2014, The Dispatch reported that 56,000 children in Ohio have an incarcerated parent. Why aren't there skills trainings for these parents too?


We've invested millions of dollars in prisons and locked up thousands of Ohioans, but researchers agree it has done little to improve our crime rates.


Meghana Kudrimoti, Columbus
