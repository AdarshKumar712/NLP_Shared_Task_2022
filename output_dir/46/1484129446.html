Trump Deploys More Troops To Mideast After US Embassy Attack

Posted 9:18 am, January 1, 2020, by Associated Press

Share this:

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Pinterest
  • LinkedIn
  • Email
  • SMS

WASHINGTON (AP) — President Donald Trump has ordered about 750 U.S. soldiers deployed to the Middle East with about 3,000 more preparing for possible deployment in the next several days.

The troop movement comes after Tuesday’s attack on the U.S. Embassy in Iraq by dozens of Iran-supported militiamen. U.S. Marines were immediately sent from Kuwait to reinforce the compound.

In announcing the new deployment, Defense Secretary Mark Esper didn’t specify where the infantry battalion from the Army’s 82nd Airborne Division at Fort Bragg, North Carolina, would be heading.

But a U.S. official tells The Associated Press they’ll go to Kuwait and a full brigade of about 4,000 soldiers may eventually be sent.
