Pa. gaming revenue up more than 13% in November

Pa. gaming revenue up more than 13% in November

  • Reading Eagle
  • Dec 31, 2019
  • Dec 31, 2019
  • Comments

  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email

Total gaming and fantasy game revenue in the state was up more than 13% in November, according to the Pennsylvania Gaming Control Board.

When compared to November 2018, revenue at the state's 12 casinos, along with fantasy contests and video gaming, went up 13.17% to $292.09 million. Total tax revenue was $119.24 million

- Advertisement -

The two casinos run by Wyomissing-based Penn National Gaming Inc.both saw increased year-over-year revenue. Hollywood Casino at Penn National  Race Course in Dauphin County was up 9.13% to $21.08 million and The Meadows Casino in Washington County was up 4.96% to $20.76 million.

Internet games contributed $9.6 million with Hollywood Casino pitching in $1.86 million to that total. Penn National's sports books brought in a combined $21.82 million — the Meadows had $18.06 million and Hollywood Casino had $3.76 million.

Marquee by Penn video game terminals, which is operated by Penn National and located in some Rutter's convenience stores, had gross revenue of $541,158 in November. Overall VGT revenue was $659.288.
