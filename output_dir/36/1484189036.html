Gallery: Tulsa World stories of the decade

  • By Kendrick Marshall Tulsa World

    Kendrick Marshall

    Assistant Editor

How much can happen in a decade?

A lot.

For the first time in Oklahoma history, voters elected a woman to lead the state. There was a record snowstorm, flooding, earthquakes and devastating tornadoes.

High-profile law enforcement shootings led to calls for institutional reform and an examination of whether race factored into policing. 

A professional basketball team the state grew to love unexpectedly reached the NBA Finals. On the gridiron, Oklahoma football produced two Heisman Trophy winners with unique backstories.

The state lost a billionaire benefactor deeply enamored with orange and black. A tragic plane crash and homecoming parade wreck shook Oklahoma State University. A gut-wrenching murder in a quiet Broken Arrow neighborhood tested the resolve of a community.   

Teachers who demanded fair wages and support left their classrooms to fight for education in Oklahoma. And a city wanting to come to grips with its difficult past vowed to unearth truths by exploring cemeteries where secrets might be kept. 

Here is a look back at some of the more memorable stories in Oklahoma over the past 10 years.

2010: ONEOK Field opens in downtown Tulsa

2010: Mary Fallin voted Oklahoma's first female governor

2011: Tulsa Police Department corruption trial

2011: Record winter storm paralyzes Tulsa

2011: Earthquakes rattle Oklahoma

2011: Oklahoma State University plane crash

2012: Good Friday shootings in Tulsa

2012: Creek County wildfires

2012: Oklahoma City Thunder reach NBA Finals

2013: Powerful tornado rips through Moore

2013: Fairmont Terrace quadruple homicide

2014: Oklahoma Department of Corrections botches execution of Clayton Lockett

2014: Same-sex Marriage in Oklahoma

2014: Kevin Sweat pleads guilty in Weleetka girls', fiancee's killings

2014: Tulsa Police Officer Shannon Kelper kills 19-year-old Jeremey Lake; gets 15-year prison sentence

2015: Tulsa County Reserve Deputy Robert Bates shoots Eric Harris

2015: Tulsa County Sheriff Stanley Glanz resigns amid criminal indictments

2015: Oklahoma State University homecoming parade crash

2015: Bever family murders

2016: The shooting of Terence Crutcher

2016: Buddy Hield has historic season; leads OU to Final Four

2017: Bob Stoops announces retirement

2017: Baker Mayfield, once a walk-on at OU, wins Heisman

2018: Teachers walk out, demand higher pay from lawmakers

2018: Gathering Place opens in Tulsa

2018: Voters approve medical marijuana in Oklahoma

2018: Kyler Murray becomes Sooners' seventh Heisman Trophy winner

2019: Flooding in Oklahoma

2019: Oilman, billionaire Oklahoma State University donor T. Boone Pickens dies

2019: David Boren steps down as OU president; successor leaves less than a year later

2019: Search for graves related to 1921 Tulsa Race Massacre begins

2019: 462 Oklahoma inmates released
