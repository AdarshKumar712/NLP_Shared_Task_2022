Buy Local campaign making a difference

  • 7 hrs ago

By Dave Russell

 

With calls to “Buy Local!” pealing from seemingly every corner, Sylva business owners report mostly positive results during the Christmas season.

Steve Baxley, owner of Baxley’s Chocolates, reported a steadier stream of customers in December than in past years.

“We certainly had good business through the season,” he said. “One thing that was different – and I am wondering it was connected to the Buy Local campaign – is that usually we have this mad rush around Christmas Eve or the day before, when we are swarmed. It was more spread out this year.”

Harry Alter, who owns a bookstore of the same name, said he didn’t see a Christmas bump, but he appreciates the buy local campaign.

“People are filtering back in from traveling, so I am expecting it to pick up,” he said.

Megan Hall, manager of Balsam Mountain Outdoors, reports a “super great” season.

“We like to see locals who shop local and we get them throughout the year and especially Christmas time,” she said. “People are like ‘This year, I’m trying to strictly buy local for my family.’ It helps us and it helps our community.”

Violetta Ohliger, owner of Sassy Frass Consignment, said she had an “excellent” season overall though it was quiet around Christmas.

“It was different from last year,” she said. “These past two weeks were quiet. Last year people were more interested in decorating and they loved jewelry and candles and stuff like that. People love being downtown and they love the changes in the store.”

Sylva’s three-day Christmas celebration Dec. 6-8 brought more locals downtown, she said.

Ohliger is optimistic for the future.

“This next year looks promising,” she said. “People love downtown. People have bought new homes and they come down here from everywhere to shop.”

At the Farmhouse Mercantile and Coffee Bar, owner Tammy Fuller said her Christmas season was “phenomenal.”

“It was beyond what we ever thought, dreamed or imagined it could be,” she said.

Her customers came from near and far, she said.

“And we had them from age 5 all the way up to 95,” she said. “The locals have really enjoyed the new coffee shop and this whole new experience.”

There are good reasons to buy from friends and neighbors instead of sending money off far away. A consumer spending $100 in a locally owned business keeps $68 in Sylva and Jackson County. That same $100 spent at a national chain only sees $43 put back into the local economy, according to Paige Dowling, Sylva town manager.
