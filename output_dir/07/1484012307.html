Australian troops prepare for bushfire emergency evacuations by sea

A major humanitarian crisis is threatening people in the NSW South Coast, with people given a 24 deadline to leave. It comes as food and water supplies dwindle and the Australian Defence Force is called in to help.

Bushfires continue to create havoc along the east coast

Authorities have less than 24 hours to move the thousands of people stranded on the NSW South Coast who face a humanitarian crisis amid looming horror fire conditions.  

Bushfire-ravaged communities in southern NSW will continue to try to assess the damage after seven lives and at least 176 homes were lost.

In Victoria, residents are coming to grips with “horrifying” bushfires which have destroyed properties and claimed a life in East Gippsland, as firefighters continue to fend off blazes.

And, a Catastrophic Fire Danger Rating has been issued for Western Australia’s Goldfields region today.

Saturday 'could be worse' than Wednesday

Benjamin Graham

It's being warned that conditions on Saturday could be worse than those on New Year’s Eve when aggressive bushfires tore through communities causing widespread destruction.

“There is every potential that the conditions on Saturday will be as bad or worse than we saw yesterday (Tuesday),” Rural Fire Service Deputy Commissioner Rob Rogers told reporters in Sydney.

In NSW, winds are expected to be very strong with temperatures forecast to surpass 40C, mixed with low humidity which is expected to produce a severe fire danger.

The state’s southeast, the southern ranges, the Illawarra, south coast and the central ranges are expected to experience the worst of the conditions. Authorities are also concerned about the Green Wattle Creek fire southwest of Sydney which has broken containment lines.

“Crews are working hard to get some containment before Saturday but we are concerned about that fire because of its potential run into far western Sydney,” Mr Rogers said.

Catastrophic Fire Danger in Western Australia

Benjamin Graham

A Catastrophic Fire Danger Rating has been issued for Western Australia’s Goldfields region.

AAP reports that the bushfire is continuing to burn in the area and the dry and windy conditions expected today will exacerbate the risk, the Department of Fire and Emergency Services says.

Very hot, fresh and gusty winds over the central and southeast parts of WA on Thursday as a trough moves east will increase the Fire Danger Ratings over the area, reaching #Catastrophic ratings across the #Goldfields and #Eucla. As a result, the Eyre Highway will be closed for a minimum of five days.

DFES advises people to avoid the area, ensure they have enough food and water supplies, and refer to Main Roads Travel Map to plan an alternate route.

People are also urged to stop taking back tracks and unsealed roads in an endeavour to avoid the road closures.
