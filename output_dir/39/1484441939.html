3 people dead after southeast Columbus house fire

3 people dead after southeast Columbus house fire
3 people dead after southeast Columbus house fire | WBNS-10TV Columbus, Ohio | Columbus News, Weather & Sports
(Image Source: 10TV staff)
10TV Web Staff
Published: 01/01/20 07:49 pm EST
Updated: 01/01/20 11:28 pm EST

COLUMBUS, Ohio -- Three people are dead after a house fire that happened Wednesday night in southeast Columbus.

Columbus Fire Chief Steve Martin says the fire happened in the 3300 block of Retriever Road. A call about the fire was received at 7:25 p.m.

Fire officials say the bodies of two victims were found on the first floor of the home.

According to authorities, two adults and three children live at the home. Two of the children have been found and are safe.

Based on observations made by firefighters when they arrived at the scene, it's believed there may have been some type of an explosion.
